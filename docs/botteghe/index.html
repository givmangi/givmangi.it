Trentino-Alto Adige is the second region when it comes to sales volume per capita in the whole of Italy, and it makes it difficult for antique shops to keep up with the multi-million businesses that invest on this territory.

So, how do you protect them?

In 2011, the municipality of Trento created the program "Botteghe storiche trentine" in order to make historical shops more recognizable and to protect them from the arising competition in the province

Historical shops are considered to be those retail establishments in fixed premises, public establishments serving food and beverages, and craft enterprises (selling their own products), even if they are located outside the historic places of commerce, which meet the following two requirements:

In order to obtain the plate which certificates and shows the membership of the register of historical shops, the enterprise has to submit the application for recognition as historical shop and consequent registration in the Register to the territorially competent municipality

There is one municipality that greatly stands out

These graphs shows us one particular tourist attraction: it is the municipality of Primiero San Martino di Castrozza. This municipality was established on 1 January 2016 by the merger of Fiera di Primiero, Siror, Tonadico and Transacqua.

The particular thing for which Primiero stands out is that, while it is a pretty strong tourist attraction, it goes against most trends in many metrics: while most valleys in Trentino are full of empty shops that are closing one after the other, here there's one of the strongest shops' "survival rates" in the province, and while the whole of Trentino is trying to attract foreigners, Primiero is alright with having the lowest foreigners' rate in the province

But how can it be?

Primiero San Martino di Castrozza is a small municipality that is fine with not renewing their culture, and as we can see they don't even tend to attract new investments from outside, with the highest rate in the whole of Trentino for square kilometers for GDO (Big Distribution Chains)
